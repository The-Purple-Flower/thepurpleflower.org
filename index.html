How We Help Survivors Escape & Rebuild

We provide critical services to help survivors navigate crisis, find safety, and rebuild their lives free from violence. Wherever you are in your survivor journey, we're here to reconnect you to safety, options, and yourself—so you can rebuild.

24/7 Crisis Hotline

Immediate support, safety planning, and resources available day or night when you need it most.

Emergency Shelter Assistance

We coordinate safe, temporary lodging options and transportation for families in crisis.

Support groups, counseling referrals, and trauma-informed classes designed to re-empower survivors.

Job & Housing Advocacy

Assistance with employment and housing applications and financial independence planning.

Our Story

On Dec 31, 2012, a young mother, Laura Aceves, lost her life trying to escape her abuser. She was murdered, with her 4 month old child left by her side. At that time, we learned there were no services for victims in the county.

The Purple Flower grew out of a response to Laura's death, so that no more people would be without help in Carroll County, AR. We offer support to anyone experiencing domestic abuse and/or sexual assault.

Established in 2014, The Purple Flower provides resources, support, services, and empowerment to those who have been affected by domestic violence, sexual assault, and/or human trafficking within Carroll County.

We also work to prevent the occurrence of domestic violence, sexual assault and human trafficking in Carroll County by providing awareness and education to the public, businesses, partners, community organizations and related support agencies in the area.
